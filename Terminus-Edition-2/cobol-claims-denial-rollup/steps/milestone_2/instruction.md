Adjustment records have the same fixed-width layout as milestone 1: type, 12-character claim id, amount, and member id only. There is no adjustment reason field to parse. An adjustment matches only when the full 12-character claim id, member id, amount, denied claim status, and the claim record's allowed denial reason line up. Keep one row per adjustment in input order with columns `claim_id,member_id,reason,amount_cents,status`; matched rows must use status `MATCHED` and show the canonical denial reason from the matched claim, and unmatched rows must use status `UNMATCHED` and leave `reason` blank.

The new legacy feed can contain claim-side reason aliases in the reason-code field of each `claims.dat` claim record: `BIL` means `COB`, `AUN` means `AUT`, and `CLN` means `NEC`. Normalize that claim record reason before matching. The complete allowed canonical reason set remains `MED`, `NEC`, `COB`, and `AUT`; milestone 2 adds aliases and must not remove `MED`. Matched report rows must emit the canonical reason (`MED`, `COB`, `AUT`, or `NEC`), not any alias from `claims.dat`; unknown claim reasons remain unmatched. Each denied claim row can be consumed by at most one adjustment. If two separate eligible claim rows share the same claim id, member id, and amount, the first two eligible adjustments may consume them in claim input order, but a third duplicate adjustment must remain `UNMATCHED`. Keep `amount_cents` as the 10-character zero-padded cents value from the adjustment record, like `0000000300`. The summary must use `key=value` lines for `matched_count`, `matched_amount_cents`, `unmatched_count`, and `unmatched_amount_cents`, with adjustment amounts counted as positive cents.
