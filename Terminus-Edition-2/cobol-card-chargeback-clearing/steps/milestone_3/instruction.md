Extend the card chargeback clearing batch in `/app/src/chargeback_clear.cbl` for a legacy merchant feed and cycle controls while preserving all prior report and summary requirements. It must still read `/app/data/sales.dat` and `/app/data/chargebacks.dat`, then write `/app/out/chargeback_report.csv` and `/app/out/chargeback_summary.txt` using the report schema and four-key summary schema from milestone 1.

For this milestone, sale records may contain legacy reason aliases in the sale reason field: `FRD` means canonical `F10`, `M20` means canonical `F20`, and `MER` means canonical `MRC`. Normalize the sale reason before matching and report the canonical reason for applied chargebacks. Unknown reasons remain exceptions.

Sale records may carry an 8-character settlement date immediately after the status byte, and chargeback records may carry an 8-character chargeback date immediately after the merchant id. Dates use `YYYYMMDD`. See `/app/docs/record_layouts.md` and `/app/docs/date_gating.md`.

When both the sale settlement date and the chargeback date are absent (legacy records that end after the status byte or merchant id), skip cycle-date gating entirely and keep milestone 1–2 matching behavior (including alias normalization, full 12-character sale id equality, row consumption, and positive applied cents). Apply cycle rules when either side carries a date. This differs from dated-length records where a date position is present but blank: a blank settlement or chargeback date in dated mode is ineligible.

When cycle rules apply, a chargeback can apply only when both dates are nonblank, both dates are listed as `OPEN` in `/app/config/cycle_calendar.txt`, the chargeback date is not before the sale settlement date (equal calendar days are eligible), and at most two `OPEN` calendar days occur strictly after the settlement date through and including the chargeback date. Dates missing from the calendar are not open.

If more than one unused settled sale row matches the same chargeback after normalization and cycle checks, choose the sale with the latest settlement date; when settlement dates tie, choose the latest sale input row. If a later chargeback repeats the same sale id, merchant, and amount, it may consume the next-latest unused matching sale row. The chargeback report must still keep chargeback input order, leave `reason` blank for `EXCEPTION` rows, preserve the fixed-width zero-padded `amount_cents`, and count applied amounts as positive cents.
