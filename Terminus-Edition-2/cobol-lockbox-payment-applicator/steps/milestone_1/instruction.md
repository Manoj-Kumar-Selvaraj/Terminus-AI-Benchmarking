The lockbox payment applicator in `/app/src/lockbox_apply.cbl` is creating bad posting files for same-day remittance batches. Lockbox-channel payments are being left unapplied, some invoice ids collide with the wrong invoice, cutoff-date payments are mishandled, and the applied total has the wrong sign.

Fix the COBOL program so it reads `/app/data/invoices.dat` and `/app/data/payments.dat`, then writes `/app/out/lockbox_report.csv` and `/app/out/lockbox_summary.txt`. A payment applies only when full invoice id, customer id, amount, open invoice status, invoice hold flag `N`, payment disposition `P`, payment date not later than the invoice cutoff date, and an allowed channel all line up. Channels `ACH`, `WIR`, `CRD`, and `LBX` are allowed. Each invoice can be applied at most once; duplicate payments for the same invoice leave only the earliest eligible payment applied.

The report must keep one row per payment in input order with columns `invoice_id,customer_id,channel,amount_cents,payment_date,status`; use `APPLIED` or `UNAPPLIED` for status and leave `channel` blank when no invoice applied. In the report, `amount_cents` should stay as the 10-character zero-padded cents value from the payment record, like `0000000750`. The summary should use `key=value` lines for `applied_count`, `applied_amount_cents`, `unapplied_count`, and `unapplied_amount_cents`, with payment amounts counted as positive cents.
